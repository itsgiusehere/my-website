# ADR-003: Three Engagement Shapes — Naming and Content

**Date:** 2026-04-28 (updated 2026-04-29)
**Status:** accepted
**Deciders:** Giuseppe, Cooper

## Context

The website previously described two engagement shapes: Visioning Project and Embedded partnership. Two Cooper sessions (2026-04-28 and 2026-04-29) surfaced that the actual range of work spans three distinct shapes. The three-shape model is now confirmed.

---

## Decisions

### 1. Three engagement shapes

| Name | Shape | Duration | Examples |
|------|-------|----------|---------|
| **Concept Sprint** | Compressed exploration in service of strong convergence. Takes a known problem and produces a concept the team can build from. | Weeks | Sennder (redefining a broken key experience), SumUp (to-be onboarding) |
| **Visioning Project** | Extended exploration of a future landscape. Starts not from a problem but from a domain — scanning what's changing, framing what will be worth designing for. | Months | Radiation clinic (future of the digital layer in cancer treatment) |
| **Embedded partnership** | Ongoing. Holds direction across delivery. | Months, sometimes across initiatives | (unchanged) |

### 2. Naming rationale

- **Concept Sprint** replaces the current "Visioning Project" name. "Sprint" signals compression and urgency — moving toward something fast. "Concept" names the deliverable. The contrast between Sprint (compressed, convergent) and Project (sustained, exploratory) does real work without needing explanation.
- **Visioning Project** moves to the futures/foresight engagement. "Visioning" signals that direction is still being found, not yet clear. "Project" signals it is bounded — a defined start, end, and deliverable. Previously retired as a name because it implied short duration; that problem is resolved now that the sprint engagement takes its own name.
- **Embedded partnership** unchanged.

## Content Direction for Tiziano

### Concept Sprint

The current Visioning Project copy is the starting point. It needs a Tiziano pass to bring in the sprint quality — compression, intensity, moving toward something with urgency. The subtitle and body may work largely as-is; the tone needs to carry the sprint signal.

**Current subtitle (to review):**
> Turning half-formed direction or an emerging opportunity into a concept the team can build from.

**Current body (to review):**
> The work runs through understanding, reframing, opening up options, and converging on a direction. Conversations with users, PMs, marketers, engineers become insights, journey maps, experience flows. What lands is a UX concept, a product vision, or the design of a customer experience across channels — co-created with the team and concrete enough to keep testing and building from.

**Duration label:** Weeks to months (review — may simplify to "Weeks")

---

### Visioning Project (new — needs full copy pass from Tiziano)

**Duration label:** Months

**Subtitle:**
> Understanding where a domain is heading — and what it will be worth designing for.

**Body:**
> The work starts with inquiry. Expert interviews, company strategy sessions, desk research — building a picture of how the domain is evolving and what futures are emerging. That picture gets structured: the domain is mapped, value chains and journeys traced, key tensions and opportunities identified.
>
> What-if questions open the generative phase. Co-creation sessions and brainstorming produce early concepts — not deliverables yet, but probes: each one tests whether the projections about the future are sound, and whether we're focused on the right problems and value opportunities.
>
> What lands is a set of design definitions — each concept described by what it does, the problem it solves, and the benefit it delivers — held together by a narrative that puts them in motion and makes the direction vivid enough to act on.

**What Tiziano should know:**
- This is content direction, not final copy. Voice and rhythm need a full pass.
- The distinction from Concept Sprint is that exploration is the dominant work here — concepts are instruments for testing projections, not deliverables in themselves (until the final phase).
- "What-if questions" is a real method term. Tiziano to judge whether it stays or gets softened.
- "A narrative that puts them in motion" refers to a day-in-the-life-of-the-future format. The method name was deliberately dropped from the copy — the description should stand on its own.

## Context Not Included in Copy (For Future Reference)

The following was discussed and understood but deliberately kept out of the website copy. Recorded here in case it becomes relevant later.

**Giuseppe's role in Visioning Project engagements:**
He works inside a team of researchers, designers, and strategists — sometimes an existing client team, sometimes one he assembles. His specific value in that context: holding the full breadth of the research (he loves research, formulating hypotheses, questioning projections) and synthesising it into convergent design direction. He is the person who keeps the line clear between what's emerging in the landscape and what it means for the design. This was left out of the copy because the team composition varies by engagement and is operational detail that belongs in a scoping conversation, not the website.

**The "multiple hats" differentiator:**
In a team of specialists, Giuseppe spans research, design, facilitation, product development, and project management. Being a generalist with 15+ years across disciplines is what makes him suited to this kind of work. Not included in the copy — the body implies it through the arc, and stating it explicitly risks reading as a CV line.

**Client profile for Visioning Project engagements:**
Commissioned by Marketing VPs. Design VPs act as sponsor and bridge. Design Managers are typically the hiring decision-maker. This is a different commissioning chain from the Concept Sprint and Embedded partnership clients (Design VPs, Heads of Design making their own hiring decisions). Not addressed in the copy — the website currently speaks to one audience, and adding a second creates positioning complexity that hasn't been resolved.
