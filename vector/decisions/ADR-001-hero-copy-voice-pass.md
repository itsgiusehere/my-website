# ADR-001: Hero headline and subheadline rewritten for voice

**Date:** 2026-04-14
**Status:** accepted
**Deciders:** Giuseppe, Tiziano

## Context

The Hero subheadline was flagged in VECTOR as pending a Tiziano pass. Reading it end-to-end, two issues surfaced:

1. The subheadline opened with *AI makes building easy — and easily scattered. Direction is scarce.* This was a 2026 content-marketing framing, not Giuseppe's voice, and it ages badly. It also delayed the real line (the altitude-range differentiator) by two sentences.
2. The headline — *I turn fragmented thinking into shared clarity — so your teams can move forward* — stacked two abstractions (*fragmented thinking* → *shared clarity*) without concrete anchoring, and used an em dash where a comma would do.

Separately, neither line named the *what* — the domain the work applies to — which meant the next section's intro had to carry that weight.

## Decision

**Headline:**
> I turn scattered thinking into a direction teams can move on.

**Subheadline:**
> I zoom out to frame the direction of a digital product or experience, and zoom in to make it tangible. The craft is in doing both, and bringing everyone along.

The AI opener is dropped. The altitude idea (zoom out / zoom in / bringing everyone along) opens the subheadline directly. *Digital product or experience* names the domain in the subheadline without formatting emphasis.

## Consequences

**Positive**
- Hero now answers "what does he do" within the first two visible lines.
- Doctrine is preserved: personal voice, concrete over abstract, no trend language.
- The bridge section between The Problem and How I Help can drop its situation-restating clause, since the Hero now carries the domain. Reduces redundancy across the top half of the page.

**Negative / to watch**
- Loss of the *Direction is scarce* aphorism, which some readers found punchy. Trade accepted: punchy-and-trendy costs more than it earns.
- The headline is slightly longer than the previous version. Bulk to confirm it still typesets cleanly at mobile widths.

## Alternatives Considered

- **Keep the AI opener, tighten the rest.** Rejected — the opener is the core problem, not the rest.
- **Move the *what* (digital product or experience) into the headline.** Rejected — flatter phrasing (*product direction*) loses the scope that *direction of a digital product or experience* carries.
- **Bold *digital product or experience* in the subheadline for emphasis.** Rejected — bolding inside body copy is scaffolding; the phrase already stands out by contrast as the only concrete noun in the sentence.
- **Aphoristic subheadline without "I"** (*Framing direction takes zooming out. Making it tangible takes zooming in.*). Rejected — site doctrine is personal not corporate; the I-voice is truer to the page.
