## Core Beliefs

### Why I went independent

> To have impact faster. Working across industries lets me cross-pollinate approaches that teams inside one company rarely see — and staying outside internal politics means I can focus on what actually moves things forward.

### What I believe

1. **People-centredness, not just user-centredness** — Everyone commits to a vision because it serves their interest. Understanding what each stakeholder contributes *and* what they get out of it is key — not just for users, but for everyone involved internally.

2. **Trust through action, not words** — Trust comes from active listening, identifying quick wins, and building foundational items where design isn't a bottleneck. Show progress, don't just promise it.

3. **Speed through tight feedback loops** — Work offline, create, share quickly, get feedback, iterate. Quick sparring beats long cycles.

4. **Always recommend a next step** — No matter how complex the project, always provide a recommendation. Without clear next steps, we stay stuck.

5. **Validate only what matters** — Don't get stuck validating everything. Make assumptions, reuse what exists, focus validation on what truly matters for progress.

6. **A tangible vision enables collective action** — Innovation happens when humans come together around a shared vision they believe in. A key visual and well-crafted message can enable progress quickly.

7. **Soft skills are prerequisites, not nice-to-haves** — The human factor — gaining trust, active listening, understanding motivations — is key to achieving any progress. Without it, even the best strategy fails.

### Synthesised POV

> Progress happens when people align around something they can see and believe in. My job is to create that clarity — and to understand what each stakeholder needs to fully commit.
>
> I don't validate everything; I focus on what really matters. I build trust through quick wins and visible progress, not promises. And I always recommend a next step — without clear direction, we stay stuck.

## Working Process

### Four phases

1. **Diving & Sense-Making** — deep dive into the problem domain, desk research, stakeholder interviews, existing documentation. Critical for gaining systemic understanding.
2. **Framing & Clarifying** — rapid iterations and close collaboration to define the problem, challenge assumptions, gain new perspectives, identify quick wins.
3. **Envisioning & Designing** — working inclusively and iteratively with stakeholders to envision desired solutions and outcomes.
4. **Delivering & Iterating** — bridging gaps between current state and envisioned future. Highly iterative; may involve additional research and ideation.

### Problem exploration framework

Five-step approach for exploring problems thoroughly:

1. **Observations** — visible, concrete manifestations of the problem
2. **Perspectives** — stakeholder roles and viewpoints
3. **Levers** — aspects that influence the problem and their interconnections
4. **Themes** — key stories emerging from research
5. **Focus** — areas of influence where small changes create bigger impact
